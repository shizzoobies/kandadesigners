Wrap up

Carry these habits into real messages

You reviewed several emails and texts, including phishing attempts and a normal internal message, and selected responses that keep information where it belongs.

  • Look at who a message is really from, not just the display name or contact label.
  • Notice warnings, links, and attachments that do not match normal internal patterns.
  • Keep customer and payment details inside approved tools instead of replying with them or sending screenshots.
  • Never share your Employee ID, password, or security verification codes, and do not start screen sharing or install a "security fix" from an unexpected message.

Those extra seconds you spend checking a message can prevent data exposure and protect customers. If you are ever unsure, stop and check with your supervisor or your security point-of-contact before you act.
